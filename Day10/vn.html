Vegetarian Food:-

pasta

Veggie pasta (zucchini, spaghetti squash, eggplant and butternut squash) Whether ribboned, shredded, scraped or julienned, freshly made vegetable-based pasta is an excellent way to boost your veggie intake and reduce carbs. “If you're aiming for fewer carbohydrates and calories, try veggie pastas,”

Substituting veggie pasta for traditional varieties can indeed provide a health benefit. It may not be as pronounced as swapping out your pasta entirely for a spaghetti squash, but the added benefit of not needing to take the time to prepare vegetables ahead of time is a definite win for pasta

Salad

Vegetable salads may be marinated or sauced mixtures of raw or cooked vegetables. They are commonly based on tomatoes, green beans, cucumbers, beets, and mushrooms. Cole slaw (from the Dutch kool, “cabbage”) is made of shredded or chopped cabbage with a mayonnaise or vinegar-based dressing.

Salad greens contain Vitamin A, Vitamin C, beta-carotene, calcium, folate, fiber, and phytonutrients (see Table 1). Leafy vegetables are a good choice for a healthful diet because they do not contain cholesterol and are naturally low in calories and sodium.

Fish fingers

Fish fingers are processed fish product prepared using fish meat, which has been battered and breaded. Fish fingers are regular sized portions cut from rectangular frozen blocks of fish fillet or fish mince.

Fish fingers can be a healthier option than other convenience foods like burgers or pizza, but they are usually coated and fried, so they aren't the healthiest way to eat fish. They can be high in nutrients like vitamin A, iodine, zinc, omega-3 fatty acids, protein, selenium, and vitamin B-12. Omega-3 fatty acids can help with brain development and function in children.
